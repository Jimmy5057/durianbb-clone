Mao Shan Wang FAQ

What is Musang King durian?

The Musang King durian is a well-known and perhaps one of the most-consumed varieties of durian. It is oval-shaped and has a lighter green husk.

The Musang King durian smells sweet and doesn’t hit your nose with a sharp punch. The texture of its flesh is thick, delicate, rich, buttery, in bright golden yellow, and comes off easily from its flat, baby seed.

Musang King is one of the durians with multiple layers of taste! It is worth trying at least once. Musang King is, without a doubt, one of the most popular durians in Malaysia.

What does Musang King taste like?

The Mao Shan Wang or Durian Musang King is known for its unusual combination of sweet and savoury taste, with a hint of bitterness lingering on the tongue. In addition to its distinctive taste, the durian musang king is notable for its delicate, rich, and buttery texture.

Because of their unique flavour profile, the Mao Shan Wang or Durian Musang King are often considered to be the “kinder surprise” of the durian world and one of the best durian to eat. If you’re looking for a truly unique culinary experience, the durian musang king is definitely worth trying!

What are the signs of a good quality durian Musang King?

If you are on the hunt for a good quality Musang King durian during the durian season in Malaysia, here are some of the signs to keep an eye out for:

  • A strong and distinctive aroma. The stronger the aroma, the more likely it is to be a ripe and flavourful fruit.
  • Rich golden-yellow flesh. Avoid those with pale or overly dark flesh, they may not be as ripe or flavourful.
  • Firm, creamy texture. The flesh should not be too dry or too wet.
  • Small seeds: The Musang King durian is known to have small seeds compared to other durian varieties.
  • No off-putting odours and signs of spoilage: Avoid durians with mouldy or rotten spots on the skin as well as those with off-putting odours; they might be overripe or spoiled!

How do I store the Musang King durians properly upon delivery if I'm unable to consume them immediately?

Though durians are best consumed when it is at the peak of their readiness, here are some steps you can follow to store the fruit properly:

  1. Keep them at room temperature. Avoid exposing them to direct sunlight or heat.
  2. If you need to store them for a longer period of time, refrigerate them. Wrap the durians in an airtight container to minimise the odour transfer. They can be stored in the refrigerator for 3 – 5 days.
  3. Before eating, take them out and leave them at room temperature. Durians are best eaten when they are not too cold.
